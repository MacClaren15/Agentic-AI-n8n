# Agentic-AI-n8n
Heicoder Generative AI course which helps student like myself to understand and build AI Agents for autonomous task.

Course Summary: GA100 - Generative AI Fundamentals
Over the course of 6 sessions, the following key areas were covered:

1. GenAI Fundamentals
Introduction to Generative AI: Welcome, What is AI vs. GenAI, and key differences.

History and Evolution of AI: AI, Machine Learning (ML), Neural Networks, Deep Learning, GenAI, and LLMs.

Why Generative AI Matters: Real-world applications and future opportunities.

2. Generative AI Tools
Chatbots: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot.

Core Features: Memory, Saved Memories, Chat History Reference, and Custom Instructions.

Search Tools: Traditional Search vs. AI Search, and Deep Research.

Media Generation: * Images: Sora, Nano Banana.

Audio: ElevenLabs, Suno.

3. Prompt Engineering
Fundamentals: What is a prompt?

Prompt Components: Task, Context, Persona, Format, Example, Tone, and Constraints.

Advanced Prompting Techniques:

Few-shot and Zero-shot Prompting

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

Tree-of-Thought Prompting

Flipped Interaction

4. Business Workflows (From No-Code to AI)
Data Analysis with ChatGPT: Uploading, querying, and analyzing data.

Business Workflows Evolution:

Manual Era

Automated Era

LLM-Enhanced Automation

Agentic Workflows

AI Workflow Platform: n8n

5. AI Agents and Manus AI
What are AI Agents? ReAct Cycle, differences from LLMs, and their importance.

Manus AI: Introduction, capabilities, multimodal intelligence, automation, and integrations.

Workflows with Manus AI: Data Analysis, Web Scraping, Content Creation, etc.

6. Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
Introduction: What is RAG and why it matters.

Core Concepts: Tokenization, Embeddings, Cosine Similarity, and Relevance Ranking.

Integration: Workflows and APIs (OAuth setup, Google Drive API, credentials flow).

7. Model Context Protocol (MCP)
What is MCP? History and evolution.

Technical Implementation and Architecture.

Layers and Ecosystem.

Impact and future directions.

8. Responsible AI in Practice
Technical Reliability: Addressing Hallucinations, Prompt Injection, and Data Exposure.

Safety: Designing guardrails for real-world safety.

Governance to Action: Testing, Oversight, and Incident Readiness.

Leadership: AI Leadership and Culture.
